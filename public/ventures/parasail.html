The Problem

Healthcare Revenue Cycle
Is Broken by Design

When a physician sees a patient, they may not receive payment for 90 to 120 days—or ever. Payers deny claims, require resubmissions, and delay adjudication in ways that are often indistinguishable from bad faith. This creates a systemic cash flow crisis across the provider ecosystem.

The data problem underneath is worse than the cash flow problem. Claims are submitted in EDI 837 format—a specification so arcane it requires specialists just to read it. Denials come back in 835 remittance format. Neither machine nor human can process these at scale without a data infrastructure layer purpose-built for the task.

The Data Layer Problem

The Architecture spent a decade at Change Healthcare structuring $120B+ in annual claims data. That experience revealed the root cause: the data layer—EDI pipelines, clearinghouse schemas, payer adjudication logic—is where the money disappears. Fix the data, fix the cash flow.

Denied claims annually in the US

A massive portion of submitted healthcare claims are denied on first submission—most due to data quality issues, not clinical ones.

Even clean claims wait 3–4 months for payment. Providers must finance the gap with expensive credit facilities or delay vendor payments.

Of every dollar spent on healthcare administration, a significant portion is spent fighting the data layer rather than delivering care.

The Solution

Intelligent Origination
Infrastructure

Parasail Health built the origination network layer that bridges the gap between claims submission and payment remittance—using claims data intelligence to predict payment timing, de-risk receivables, and accelerate cash flow for providers.

Claims Intelligence Layer

EDI 837/835 parsing at clearinghouse scale to predict adjudication outcomes, payment timing, and denial probability before the payer responds. The data is the underwriting model.

Liquidity Bridge

Advances against clean, adjudicated receivables so providers access cash in days rather than months. Risk-priced against the data model, not credit scores.

HIPAA-Native Architecture

Every layer built HIPAA-compliant from the ground up. PHI never leaves the compliance boundary. Audit logs are structural, not optional.
