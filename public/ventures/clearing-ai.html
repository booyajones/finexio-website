Why It Is
Inevitable

The ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware attack on Change Healthcare didn't just disrupt a company — it exposed that 1 in 3 US patient records flow through a single COBOL-era architecture owned by a $470B insurance conglomerate. The entire US healthcare claims infrastructure had a single point of failure. And it failed.

Post-breach, CMS proposed new rules requiring payer redundancy in claims processing. Congress held hearings. Every health system CFO discovered they had no alternative clearinghouse route. The regulatory and market demand for a resilient replacement has never been higher.

Technical Architecture

Distributed by Design.
Intelligent by Default.

ClearingAI is not a "better Change Healthcare." It's a fundamentally different architecture — multi-region, zero-trust, AI-first — that makes the February 2024 attack scenario structurally impossible.

01

Ingest & Validate

Real-time X12 EDI ingestion at sub-100ms. AI validates transaction structure, provider credentials, and payer eligibility simultaneously at submission.

02

AI Fraud Screen

Every claim scored against 200+ fraud signals. Pattern detection across claim bundles, provider billing history, and population-level anomaly detection. Flags before routing.

03

Intelligent Route

Dynamic routing across 5+ regional processing nodes. If one node degrades, traffic reroutes automatically in <500ms. No manual failover, no human intervention required.

04

Confirm & Log

Cryptographically signed acknowledgements (999, 277CA). Immutable audit log across all nodes. Full transaction replay capability for compliance and dispute resolution.
