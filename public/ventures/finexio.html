The Problem

AP Departments Were
Burning Money

Enterprise accounts payable was stuck in 1985. Paper checks, manual reconciliation, multi-day clearing windows, and zero yield. For Fortune 1000 companies paying thousands of suppliers monthly, this represented hundreds of millions in pure friction and lost opportunity.

The existing "solutions" required suppliers to adopt new portals, banks to build custom integrations, and AP teams to manage exceptions manually. Nobody was solving the structural problem at the data layer.

The 0-to-1 Story

What Building from Zero Actually Looks Like

Building Finexio from concept to $11B in volume required solving three distinct problems simultaneously: regulatory compliance, bank-grade data schema design, and the chicken-and-egg of two-sided network effects (buyers need suppliers, suppliers need buyers).

The insight was to solve the buyer side first—deploy without supplier friction using silent enrollment—then build the supplier experience on top of proven volume. This inverted the typical approach and removed the biggest adoption barrier in B2B payments.

1
Schema First

Built the regulatory-compliant data schema before writing a single line of UI code. The schema is the product.

2
Bank Partner Network

Structured bank partnerships to access virtual card issuance and ACH rails without building a bank. Asset-light at scale.

3
ERP Distribution

Embedded inside ERPs as a native module, not a standalone portal. This removed the behavioral change requirement for AP teams.

Under the Hood

Technology Stack

Core Infrastructure

A structured data model that enforces payment routing rules, compliance constraints, and audit trails across all transaction types. The schema validates pre-flight.

Integration Layer

ERP Native Connectors

Pre-built, certified integrations for SAP, Oracle Fusion, NetSuite, Dynamics 365, and Sage Intacct. AP files are parsed without middleware.

Payments Rails

Multi-Rail Orchestration

Intelligent routing across Visa/Mastercard virtual card, ACH (same-day and standard), and check-to-digital conversion. Rail selection is automated by yield optimization.

Compliance

PCI DSS Level 1, SOC2 Type II, NACHA compliance, and bank-grade AML/KYC built into the transaction schema. Compliance is structural, not bolted on.

Data & AI

Machine learning models score each payment for optimal rail selection, fraud risk, and yield potential. Models retrain on transaction outcomes continuously.
