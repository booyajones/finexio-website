Inevitable

UnitedHealth Group has a 14–15% administrative cost ratio. The world-class benchmark is 5–6%. On $370B in annual premium revenue, that gap represents $33B+ in excess administrative spending per year. Prior auth, manual claims review, and denial management are the largest components. AI can eliminate most of this.

CMS final rules (2024) now require payers to process prior auth requests within 7 days for non-urgent cases and 72 hours for urgent — down from 30+ days. Anthem and UHG are facing massive class-action suits over AI-driven claim denials. The pressure is from both directions: regulatory mandates forcing faster processing AND litigation requiring more accurate, auditable decisions. Legacy systems can't satisfy both requirements simultaneously.

The Auditability Requirement

Every Decision is a Legal Document

Under CMS regulations, every prior auth denial must be documented with clinical criteria and policy basis. Under the Cures Act, patients have the right to appeal with documentation. Legacy AI systems (like UHG's NaviHealth) couldn't produce this documentation — leading to $69M+ in legal liability.

Apex generates a complete decision record for every adjudication: the exact policy language evaluated, the clinical criteria applied, the data sources used, and the decision rationale. It's not just good practice — it's legally required.
