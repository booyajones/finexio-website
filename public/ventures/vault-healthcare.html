Structural Inevitability

Why It Is
Inevitable

In every other high-stakes financial system, payment protection exists. Banks have FDIC deposit insurance. Real estate has escrow. Credit cards have chargeback protection. Healthcare — a $4.5T industry moving $370B annually between payers and providers — has absolutely nothing.

The Change Healthcare breach made this absence catastrophic in real time. Hospitals that performed surgeries, dispensed medications, and ran ICUs at full capacity suddenly couldn't collect payment. Physicians stopped taking new patients. Rural hospitals issued emergency credit lines. CMS was forced to create an emergency loan program. The absence of payment protection is now a documented public health risk.

$14B+ in provider payments frozen without warning, without recourse
AHA, March 2024 — Providers had zero legal protection, zero contractual bridge payment obligation from payers
CMS issued $2B+ in emergency accelerated payments — a crisis measure, not a system
HHS Emergency Program, March 2024 — Government had to backstop what the industry had no mechanism for
Average health system holds 20–30 days of cash on hand — insufficient for 60-day disruptions
Healthcare Financial Management Association — Revenue cycle disruptions of 45+ days are existential for small providers
Every health system CFO is now mandated to address payment continuity risk
HFMA Post-Breach Guidelines 2024 — Board-level risk discussions now require a payment continuity plan

The Market Signal

Every health system CFO who watched colleagues scramble for credit lines in March 2024 is now asking one question: "What is our payment continuity plan?" There is no product that answers that question. Vault is the answer.

Product Architecture

The Vault Layer.
Always On. Always Funded.

Vault sits between payers and providers as a neutral financial intermediary. Payers pre-fund a rolling reserve. Providers receive automatic bridge payments when disruptions are detected. The model generates yield on the float, creating a self-funding protection pool.

Top Layer
Payer (UHG, Anthem, BCBS)
Pre-funds rolling 30-day payment reserve into Vault
Vault Layer (The Product)
Vault Healthcare
AI monitors claim flow, detects disruptions, auto-triggers bridge payments, generates yield on reserve float
Bottom Layer
Provider (Hospital, Clinic, Practice)
Receives guaranteed payment within 72 hours regardless of clearinghouse status
01

Disruption Detection

AI monitors real-time claim acknowledgement rates across all clearinghouse connections. Detects anomalies within 15 minutes — not 72 hours when providers start missing deposits.

02

Bridge Payment Activation

When disruption is confirmed, Vault automatically disburses bridge payments to enrolled providers based on their 90-day rolling average payment pattern — no claims adjudication required during the bridge period.

03

Settlement & Float Yield

Once clearinghouse restores, Vault settles the bridge payments from payer reserves. Float generated during the reserve holding period (30–90 days) creates a self-funding yield pool. Payers earn 1–2% on their reserve capital.
