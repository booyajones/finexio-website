Structural Inevitability

Why It Is
Inevitable

Healthcare financial reconciliation is the most broken accounting system in the US economy. When a provider submits a claim, it enters a maze of systems — clearinghouse, payer adjudication, appeals engine, payment processor, banking system, provider billing system, and government registry — none of which speak to each other.

The Change Healthcare breach made this problem existential. Transaction records were lost. Providers couldn't reconcile what had been received, what was pending, and what had been voided. Months later, hospitals were still finding billing discrepancies. CMS auditors couldn't reconcile Medicare payments. The absence of a shared ledger wasn't just inefficient — it was catastrophic.

Product Architecture

One Record.
Cryptographically Verified.

LedgerHealth is not blockchain — it's a FHIR-native distributed ledger with cryptographic verification, AI normalization, and multi-party write access. Every claim event writes to LedgerHealth, creating a shared, immutable financial truth for all parties.

01

Multi-Source Ingestion

Accepts writes from any party: provider EMR, payer system, clearinghouse, payment processor, appeals engine. Each source writes its event; AI normalizes and deduplicates into the canonical record.

02

AI Normalization

LLM normalizes incoming data: resolves ICD-10 coding variations, matches NPI identifiers across systems, reconciles payment amounts, and flags discrepancies for review — all within 500ms of ingestion.

03

Cryptographic Commit

Each record is SHA-256 hashed and appended to the ledger with a timestamp. Any subsequent modification creates a new record referencing the previous hash — full chain of custody, mathematically verifiable by any party.

04

Real-Time Reconciliation

AI continuously reconciles expected vs. received payments. Flags discrepancies instantly. Auto-generates appeal documents for underpayments. Reduces 90-day reconciliation cycles to 24 hours.

Revenue Cycle Analytics

Real-time AR dashboard showing every claim status, payment received vs. expected, denial patterns by payer and code. CFO-grade financial reporting that previously required weeks of manual analysis.

CMS Compliance Records

7-year immutable record retention for Medicare/Medicaid compliance. Every audit query answers in seconds from the ledger. OIG audits that previously took months complete in hours.

Payer-Provider Reconciliation

Eliminates the monthly "he said, she said" between payer and provider over contract rates. Shared ledger shows both parties the same adjudication history, reducing dispute cycles from months to hours.
